websites

iZooky - Static

Website service designed to connect users to providers who offer online help for anything they need, spanning from home repair to medical needs.

I started interning with iZooky my first semester in school. I was the solo designer/developer for the project, and was immediately thrown into the game with limited knowledge of development. I had to learn on the go and was able to gain experience with many tools and styles until I found something the entreprenuer approved of.

Currently, the website is somewhere between a mockup and a working prototype. The primary technogies used are HTML, CSS, Bootstrap, and some jQuery/JavaScript here and there. iZooky is designed to be viewed on a desktop or laptop, but is responsive enough for tablets.

WDDM - Static/Brunch

Homepage for the Web Development and Digital Media program at Johnson County Community College.

This was my final semester project at school were I was tasked with developing a student showcase page for the Web Development program at JCCC. I had ideas on how to incorporate a small Node.js page into the project, but ran into several road blocks along the way since the original site was developed with Wordpress. After days of trying to find work-arounds for adding Node.js, custom JavaScript, and my own styling into Wordpress without using plugins, I decided on a different route. With the "client's" permission, I spent the rest of the semester converting the Wordpress site into something I could use with Brunch, a static site generator/build tool.
